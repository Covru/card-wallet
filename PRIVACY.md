# Privacy Policy — Wallet (Local Cards)

_Last updated: June 26, 2026_

Wallet ("the app") is designed to keep your data private. This policy explains
what the app does and does not do with your information.

## The short version
The app does **not** collect, transmit, sell, or share any personal data. It has
no servers, no analytics, no advertising, and no user accounts. Everything you
put into the app stays on your device.

## What the app stores, and where
- The cards you add — names, categories, numbers/barcodes, notes, and any photos
  you attach — are stored **only on your device**, in the browser/WebView's local
  database (IndexedDB). This data never leaves the device.
- The app does not create an account and does not require an email, phone number,
  or any sign-in.

## Network
After installation, the app does not make network requests. It works fully
offline. No usage data, diagnostics, or crash reports are sent anywhere.

## Camera
If you choose to use "Scan barcode," the app uses your device camera to read a
barcode locally on the device, purely to fill in a card number. Camera images are
processed on-device and are never stored or transmitted by the app. You can use
the app fully without granting camera access by typing card numbers manually.

## Backups
The "Export backup" feature creates a file on your device that you control. The
app does not upload this file anywhere. If you share or store that file, it is
handled entirely by you.

## Data deletion
You can delete any card at any time inside the app. Uninstalling the app removes
all of its locally stored data.

## Children
The app does not collect data from anyone, including children.
